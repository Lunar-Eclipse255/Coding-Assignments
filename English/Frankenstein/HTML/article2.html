Murder of Henry Clerval

Henry Clerval, son of a Businessman, died recently on the coast of Ireland. His cause of death was strangling. The main suspect was Victor Frankenstein, a friend of Henry Clerval. It was stated by one of the witnesses that, “ She [I] lived near the beach and was standing at the door of her [the] cottage, waiting for the return of the fishermen, about an hour before she [I] heard of the discovery of the body, when she [I] saw a boat with only one man in it push off from that part of the shore where the corpse was afterwards found ” (Shelley 180). After hearing the cause of death and seeing the corpse of Henry Clerval, Victor Frankenstein, became feverous with his body becoming agitated, and he began to rave. Due to this, Victor Frankenstein was temporarily imprisoned for two months, in a near death state, the trial was to recommence when he recovered. After the two months, Victor’s name was cleared due to other witnesses proving Frankenstein’s alibi.
