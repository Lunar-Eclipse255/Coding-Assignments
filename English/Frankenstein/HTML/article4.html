Cadaver Times

Genevan Scientist Gone

Victor Frankenstein, an esteemed Genevan scientist, a man with almost no family left because they were either murdered or died from grief. He has disappeared from Geneva, with few clues to his current whereabouts. The last person he spoke to was the local Genevan Magistrate, the Magistrate said that Victor spoke of a monster of his own creation, and it was this monster that killed his family. When the Magistrate stated that it would not be practical to capture this monster, it was Victor Frankenstein who said, “ That cannot be, but all that I can say will be of little avail. My revenge is of no moment to you; yet, while I allow it to be a vice, I confess that it is the devouring and only passion of my soul. My rage is unspeakable when I reflect that the murderer, whom I have turned loose upon society, still exists. You refuse my just demand; I have but one resource, and I devote myself, either in my life or death, to his destruction ” (Shelley 204). After this, there have been reports, further and further, north speaking of an eight-foot tall human, followed by a human matching Victor’s.
