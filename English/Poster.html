Time Before Time

Japan Creation Myth

“Before there was heaven and earth, there was darkness. [...] Slowly over the course of many years, the lighter and purer part drew itself away from the heavier and denser part.”

Indegenous Australian Creation Myth

“All was darkness in the time before time. The dark land was called Il-ba-lint-ja, and it possessed nothing save an endlessly tall pole coming out of the ground. It stretched from the barren land to the top of the heavens above.”

Aztec Creation Myth

“Before the earth had form, there was Coatlicue, the Earth Mother. She swam in the chaotic waters in the darkness before time.”

Egyptian Creation Myth

"Nu was the name of the dark, swirling chaos before the beginning of time. Out of these waters rose Atum; he created himself using his thoughts and the sheer force of his will. He created a hill, for there was nowhere he could stand."

I would describe the presence of this motif as a common starting to many creation myths. I find it as an intresting set up to many other motifs. It is often called before/accompanied with the Cosmic egg, Power of the Word, and/or World Tree motifs. It offers entertainment value by being a blank start that can go almost anywhere, a blank page. The core issue that it speaks to is the thought of what came before us.

The pratical explanation of this motif could be that these civilizations wanted an explanation for how they and the world came to be, and from nothing was the most logical because something must come from nothing.

The Time before Time motif is a blank start for the civilizations to ask their questions and make their answers. With a blank canvas any god, thing, or symbol can appear to help explain something at the time thought to be unexplainable.
